Here are 3 distinct directions we can take for the "Small Element" and "Expanded View". Pick your favorite!

## 1. The "Dynamic Pill" (Apple Style)
- **Small**: A sleek, black pill (120x40px). Shows "3 Active Quests".
- **Big**: The pill expands downward fluidly into a card.
- **Animation**: Smooth "slide down" effect.
- **Vibe**: Clean, modern, unobtrusive. Like iPhone's Dynamic Island.
- **Why**: Best balance of "negligible" and "useful".

## 2. The "Cyberpunk HUD" (Neon)
- **Small**: A rotating holographic ring (60x60px) â€“ current design but more data-heavy.
- **Big**: A glass pane with scanlines and neon borders.
- **Animation**: Glitch effect or scanline reveal.
- **Vibe**: Tech-heavy, immersive, glowing.
- **Why**: Fits the "Quest/Game" theme perfectly.

## 3. The "Minimalist Chip" (Notion Style)
- **Small**: A tiny square chip (40x40px) with just an icon.
- **Big**: A clean, paper-white (or dark grey) list with subtle shadows.
- **Animation**: Simple fade in/out.
- **Vibe**: Extremely quiet, professional.
- **Why**: Least distracting. Truly "negligible".

---

**My Recommendation**: **Option 1 (Dynamic Pill)** because it allows us to show *context* ("Physics HW due") without opening it, while still being tiny. And the "expand" animation feels magical.
